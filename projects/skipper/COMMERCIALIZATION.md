# Skipper — Commercialization Framework & Proprietary Layer

## The Core Distinction

The architecture is the product.
The context is proprietary.

Skipper has two layers and they must never be confused:

**Layer 1 — The System (sellable)**
The command layer. The MCP tool chain. The github/deploy/monitor pattern. The AI orchestration architecture. This is the engine. It has value to anyone running digital or physical infrastructure. This is what gets packaged and sold.

**Layer 2 — The Soul (proprietary)**
The instance that knows John. His repos, his products, his philosophy, his decision patterns, his voice, his history, his accumulated intelligence. This is not a product. This is a person. Nobody gets this.

---

## What Proprietary Actually Means

Proprietary doesn't just mean secret. It means the thing that belongs to you and creates your advantage. The architecture can be open. The proprietary layer is what you've built on top of it.

Patent protects inventions.
Proprietary protects competitive advantage.
You don't have to file anything. You just don't hand it over.

---

## The Product Line

**Skipper Core** — stays home. The John Spivey instance. Grows more valuable over time as it accumulates context. Never sold, never shared, never replicated.

**Skipper [Vertical]** — the sellable version. Same engine, different configuration. Examples:
- Skipper for Marinas — manages dock infrastructure, patrol schedules, maintenance alerts
- Skipper for Studios — manages music production workflow, file versions, session scheduling
- Skipper for Independent Builders — the closest to Core, packaged for solo founders like John

Each vertical gets the architecture. None get the soul.

---

## The Franchise Model

McDonald's sells the system — the fryers, the process, the training. They don't give away the secret sauce recipe.

Bitwerx sells the Skipper system.
John keeps the John Spivey configuration.

---

## How to Recognize the Line

Ask: if I gave this piece to a customer, would it make me less capable or just make them more capable?

- Just them → sell it
- Both → keep it

---

## The Opportunity Pattern Delegation Rule

Capabilities that compound John's personal advantage over time get classified proprietary by default. They can be reclassified as products later — but the default is keep, not sell.
